Atlas

Boston Dynamics

The Atlas robot is one of the most advanced humanoid robots ever built, designed to operate both indoors and outdoors in a variety of environments and scenarios. Originally developed for the DARPA Robotics Challenge, Atlas has evolved significantly over the years to become increasingly capable and agile.

Watch Videos Specifications

Overview

Atlas is the world's most dynamic humanoid robot, pushing the limits of whole-body mobility. Standing approximately 1.5 meters (5 feet) tall and weighing around 89 kg (196 pounds), Atlas uses sensors in its body and legs to balance and LIDAR and stereo sensors in its head to avoid obstacles, assess the terrain, help with navigation, and manipulate objects.

The robot is designed to operate in environments built for humans, navigating through complex terrain and manipulating objects with its dexterous hands. Atlas's advanced control system and state-of-the-art hardware give it the power and balance to demonstrate human-level agility, including:

  • Running
  • Jumping
  • Backflips
  • Vaulting
  • Parkour movements

Over the years, Atlas has evolved from a tethered robot requiring external power to a fully mobile, battery-powered machine capable of impressive athletic feats and fine manipulation tasks.

Development History

2015

Atlas Unplugged

A redesigned version with 75% of parts replaced, battery power, and wireless operation capability.

2016

Next Generation Atlas

Significantly smaller (1.75m to 1.5m) and lighter, with improved mobility, dexterity, and reduced noise. This version demonstrated impressive balance capabilities.

2018

Parkour Atlas

Demonstrated the ability to jump over logs and between platforms, showing remarkable agility and balance.

2020

Modern Atlas

Latest version with sophisticated control algorithms enabling gymnastics and parkour routines. Demonstrating dance moves, forward rolls, and synchronized choreography.

Capabilities

Mobility

Atlas can navigate challenging terrain, run at speeds up to 5.5 mph, jump over obstacles, and maintain balance even when pushed or when walking on uneven surfaces.

Manipulation

With dexterous hands and arms, Atlas can lift, carry, and manipulate objects in its environment, from simple tools to complex items.

Perception

Using LIDAR, stereo vision, and other sensors, Atlas builds detailed maps of its environment and can identify and track objects and obstacles.

Power

Battery-powered operation allows for untethered movement with approximately 1 hour of operation time under normal conditions.

Technical Specifications

Physical

Height 1.5 meters (5 feet)
Weight 89 kg (196 pounds)
Material Aluminum and titanium composite

Performance

Running Speed Up to 5.5 mph (8.8 km/h)
Vertical Jump Approximately 1.2 meters
Battery Life ~60 minutes of active use

Hardware

Actuators Hydraulic and electric hybrid system
Degrees of Freedom 28 DOF
Sensors LIDAR, stereo vision, IMU, force sensors

Control Systems

Computing Onboard computers with custom control software
Control Method Advanced algorithms for dynamic balance and movement
Interface Wireless control and programming

Applications

While Atlas is primarily a research platform, its capabilities point to numerous potential applications:

Disaster Response

Navigating dangerous environments to perform search and rescue operations in disaster zones too hazardous for humans.

Industrial Operations

Performing complex tasks in industrial settings, particularly in environments not designed for robots.

Healthcare Assistance

Future versions could assist in patient handling, mobility assistance, and other physically demanding healthcare tasks.

Space Exploration

Operating in extraterrestrial environments for exploration and construction tasks.
