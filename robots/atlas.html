Atlas

Boston Dynamics

The Atlas robot is one of the most advanced humanoid robots ever built, designed to operate both indoors and outdoors in a variety of environments and scenarios. Originally developed for the DARPA Robotics Challenge, Atlas has evolved significantly over the years to become increasingly capable and agile.

Overview

Atlas is the world's most dynamic humanoid robot, pushing the limits of whole-body mobility. Standing approximately 1.5 meters (5 feet) tall and weighing around 89 kg (196 pounds), Atlas uses sensors in its body and legs to balance and LIDAR and stereo sensors in its head to avoid obstacles, assess the terrain, help with navigation, and manipulate objects.

The robot is designed to operate in environments built for humans, navigating through complex terrain and manipulating objects with its dexterous hands. Atlas's advanced control system and state-of-the-art hardware give it the power and balance to demonstrate human-level agility, including:

  • Running
  • Jumping
  • Backflips
  • Vaulting
  • Parkour movements

Over the years, Atlas has evolved from a tethered robot requiring external power to a fully mobile, battery-powered machine capable of impressive athletic feats and fine manipulation tasks.

Development History

2013

Original Atlas

First introduced by Boston Dynamics for the DARPA Robotics Challenge. This version was hydraulically powered and tethered to an external power source.

2015

Atlas Unplugged

A redesigned version with 75% of parts replaced, battery power, and wireless operation capability.
